Claim Your Free College Money

A simple guide for California high school students (9th-12th grade)

You might have $500-$1,500 waiting for you!

The California Kids Investment and Development Savings Program (CalKIDS) provides money for your future education.

How to Claim Your Account

1

Check if you're eligible

Most California public school students in 9th-12th grade qualify, especially if your family receives free/reduced lunch or Medi-Cal.

2

Gather what you'll need

  • Your State Student ID Number (SSID)
  • Your date of birth
  • The County where the student attended School
3

Visit the CalKIDS website

Go to www.calkids.org and click "Claim My Account"

Screenshot of the CalKIDS homepage with "Claim My Account" button highlighted

4

Enter your information

Fill in your details exactly how they appear in school records.

Tip: Try different name variations if it doesn't find you at first.

5

Create your online account

Set up a username and password to access your funds anytime.

Note: Use a Personal Email or Phone Number when asked for one, School assigned emails will be lost after graduation!

6

Success

You have successfully signed up for a CalKids account!

Frequently Asked Questions

Make sure you entered your details properly, check with your councilor for more information.

You can obtain your SSID by asking your councilor. Sometimes it is visable on your transcript.

Most high school students qualify for $500-$1,500 depending on grade level and financial need.

You can use it for college, trade school, or other approved education expenses after high school.

If you have all your information you will not need your parents to sign up. If not, you may need to ask for help from a parent.
